JAMAICAN BARBEQUE: JERK

“Jerk” is a term used all over Jamaica which is a style of cooking native to the island. Meat (usually chicken or pork) is marinated with a variety of spices; allspice, pimento, scotch bonnet pepper, garlic, salt, sugar, ginger...just to name a few. The traditional process of “jerking” involves slow cooking meat in a barrel container for hours while poking holes in the meat so the marinade can thoroughly saturate the meat. Jerk is no longer exclusive to chicken and pork. Seafood, lamb, tofu, vegetables; if you can eat it, you can jerk it!

You can find pre-made jerk marinade in your local grocery store or specialty Caribbean market for less than $5. The most popular brands are Grace and Walkerswood. Both brands do an incredible job of making both wet and dry marinades that blend all the spices that you would normally need to buy individually saving you a lot of time and money. My granny would use both brands when she didn't have the time to cook it traditionally. You can too!

JERK CHICKEN

Ingredients

  1. 2 lbs chicken quartered and cleaned (You can also use this recipe for chicken wings as well).
  2. Sea salt and black pepper to taste
  3. 3-4 tablespoons of Grace Jerk Seasoning or Walkerswood Jerk Seasoning.
  4. 1 teaspoon of dried thyme or 2-3 sprigs of fresh thyme, chopped
  5. 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  6. 1 stalk scallion, chopped
  7. 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  8. 2 tablespoons of soy sauce

Method

Combine all ingredients in a bowl. Add chicken and completely marinate and toss. Spices should completely coat chicken. Feel free to poke holes in meat for optimal flavor and heat! Cover with a plastic wrap and chill overnight or for a minimum of 8 hours.

Build a nice, medium-hot fire over a charcoal grill. You can also add wood chips that have been soaked in cold water for 2 hours to add additional flavor. Generously toss chips over charcoal when heating grill. The best chips to use for smoking meat is oak. Next, start adding your chicken and let cook on one side for 10 minutes. Flip and repeat. Cover grill and slow cook meat, checking frequently for 1-2 hours depending on how crispy you want your chicken. After chicken is done, eat it the Jamaican way: two slices of hard dough bread with ketchup or barbeque sauce to taste.

JERK PORK

Ingredients

  1. 2 lbs boneless pork tenderloin cleaned and chopped in large pieces. You can also try this with pork belly for the extra bit of tasty fat.
  2. Sea salt and black pepper to taste
  3. 3-4 tablespoons of Grace Jerk Seasoning or Walkerswood Jerk Seasoning.
  4. 1 teaspoon of dried thyme or 1 sprig of fresh thyme, chopped
  5. 2 garlic cloves,crushed
  6. 1 stalk scallion, chopped
  7. 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  8. 2 tablespoons of soy sauce

Method

Combine all ingredients in a bowl. Add cut up pieces of pork and completely marinate and toss. Spices should completely coat meat. Feel free to poke holes in the pork for optimal flavor and heat! Cover with a plastic wrap and chill overnight OR for a minimum of 8 hours.

Build a nice, medium-hot fire over a charcoal grill. You can also add wood chips that have been soaked in cold water for 2 hours to add additional flavor. Generously toss chips over charcoal when heating grill. The best chips to use for smoking meat is oak. Next, start adding your pork and let cook on one side for 10 minutes. Flip and repeat. Cover grill and slow cook meat, checking frequently for 1-2 hours depending on how crispy you want your pork paying attention to the fat. After pork is done, eat it the Jamaican way: two slices of hard dough bread with ketchup or barbeque sauce to taste.

*Granny's Note*

You can also cook jerk in the oven if preferred. Follow the same steps for prep and cook for 2-3 hours on 350 degrees...adjusting the temperature as needed.
